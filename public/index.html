The liability

Your employees are already using AI. Your compliance system probably cannot see it.

Every day, employees paste customer records, health information, financial data, client files, and private facts into AI tools. Most companies do not know it happened until there is a screenshot, an audit, a complaint, or a lawsuit.

The usual answer is a filter that tries to catch every bad phrasing after the fact. That game cannot be won, because the ways to phrase a violation are endless. You do not need a better filter. You need a control layer that decides before the action runs.

The shift

Govern the decision, not the string.

Most AI safety tools chase wording. Gatekeeper does something simpler and stronger. Before an action runs, it asks one bounded question: is this action allowed under the rule that applies. Clean actions pass. Risky actions pause. Violations are blocked before they ever execute.

This is not another dashboard or prompt filter. It is pre-execution governance, and it produces a sealed, verifiable record of every decision.

A record you can prove. Not a blockchain.

Every decision is sealed with a cryptographic hash that includes the one before it. Change any record and the chain breaks visibly. No network, no token, no consensus overhead. Just a tamper-evident ledger of every governed action, like an enterprise firewall for AI: it enforces the rule and logs the result.

Architecture

It runs inside your environment.

Gatekeeper installs as a standalone executable on your own network. The governance layer runs locally. The answer engine runs locally. Records are sealed locally. Your data never leaves your environment, because there is no cloud in the decision path.

Prompts are not stored. Sensitive data is masked before any record is created. The audit trail proves the governance decision without preserving the private content itself. Policy packs are built from the actual rules that govern your industry.
